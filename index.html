1) Front exterior, hood closed (top to bottom)

  • Student warning light lenses (front) and strobe (if equipped) proper color and condition; housings secure.
  • Front clearance and marker lenses proper color and condition; housings secure.
  • Front reflectors present, proper color, clean, not cracked or missing; secure.
  • Windshield and seal not cracked or broken; seal has no dry rot; glass secure.
  • Wiper arms and blades not cracked or broken; blades not dry rotted; arms secure.
  • Headlight lenses clean and not cracked or loose; housings secure.
  • Front turn signal and four-way lenses proper color and condition; housings secure.
  • Exterior mirrors and brackets clean, adjusted, and secure.
  • Overall stance not leaning; level front to rear and side to side.
  • Ground/underbody no puddles or dripping fluids; no hazards around bus.

Say: I will now open the hood.

2) Hood open, engine area (fluids first, then inside out)

  • Fluids: coolant level between marks, cap present, no leaks; engine oil proper level (pull, wipe, reinsert, read), cap present; power steering fluid level proper, no leaks; listen for air leaks. If hydraulic, check brake fluid reservoir level and cap.
  • Steering system: steering box mounted with bolts, no leaks; linkage joints (pitman arm, drag link, tie-rod ends) secured with castle nuts and cotter pins, not loose or worn.
  • Front suspension: springs not cracked, bent, broken, shifted, or missing; front/rear hangers bolted; U-bolts present and secured with nuts; shocks bolted both ends, no leaks; air bags (if equipped) mounted top and bottom, no leaks.
  • Front brakes: hoses/lines no cracks, wear, frays, or leaks; fittings tight; (if visible) chamber clamped and mounted, not leaking; no brake contamination on visible linings/drums/rotors.
  • Steering axle wheel group: tire ICD (gauge inflation, no cuts/bulges, depth ≥ 4/32 in, valve stem & cap present); rim no cracks or weld repairs; all lug nuts tight (no rust trails/shiny threads); hub or axle seal not leaking.

Say: I would check the other wheel the same as this one. I would check the other suspension the same as this side.

3) Driver side exterior (top to bottom)

  • Side lenses and reflectors proper color and condition; housings secure.
  • Passenger monitoring devices (external mirrors/cameras) clean, adjusted, brackets secure.
  • Battery box: batteries held down; connections tight with no excessive corrosion; cables insulated; box/cover latched.
  • Frame and crossmembers: no cracks, broken welds, holes, or damage.

4) Driver side axles (inside out)

  • Suspension: springs not cracked, bent, broken, shifted, or missing; hangers bolted; U-bolts with nuts secure; shocks bolted both ends; air bags mounted top/bottom and not leaking.
  • Brakes: hoses/lines no cracks, wear, frays, or leaks; fittings tight; (if visible) chambers clamped/mounted; no brake contamination on visible linings/drums/rotors.
  • Wheels: tires ICD (gauge inflation, no cuts/bulges, depth ≥ 2/32 in on non-steer), rims no cracks or weld repairs, all lug nuts present/tight, axle or hub seal not leaking.

Say: I would check the other axles the same as this one.

5) Rear

  • Student warning light lenses (rear) proper color and condition; strobe if equipped; housings secure.
  • Rear lenses: tail, brake, reverse, turn, four-ways, clearance/markers proper color and condition; housings secure.
  • Rear reflectors: present, proper color, clean, not cracked or missing; secure.
  • Rear emergency door exterior: opens, locks open, then closes securely.

6) Passenger side + Fuel/DEF

  • Passenger side: I would inspect this side the same as the driver side (lenses/reflectors, monitoring devices, battery if equipped/accessible, frame).
  • Fuel tank: secured with straps/brackets and bolts; cap tight; no leaks from tank or lines.
  • DEF tank (if equipped): secured with straps; cap present; no leaks.

Say: I will now close the hood.

7) Passenger entry door and steps

  • Entry door not damaged; operates smoothly; closes securely from inside.
  • Handrails secure; step light(s) work if equipped; steps clear and tread not loose or worn.
  • Lift (if equipped) has no leaks/damage/missing parts; fully retracted and latched.

8) In-vehicle (key on) checks

  • Lighting indicators: left, right, four-ways, high beam, ABS, DEF if equipped.
  • Emergency equipment: 3 triangles; fire extinguisher charged and mounted; spare fuses or identify circuit breakers.
  • Windshield & traffic monitoring devices: windshield clean/no damage; mirrors clean/adjusted; cameras clean/operable if equipped.
  • Wipers & washers: spray and wipe; arms/blades secure.
  • Heater & defroster: identify controls; demonstrate airflow.
  • Horn(s): works.

9) Interior student area & emergency exits

  • Passenger seating: inspect one seat; frame not broken; frame attached to floor; cushion secured. Say: I would check all other seats the same as this one.
  • Emergency exits interior: labeled, not damaged, operate smoothly, close securely. Demonstrate at least one exit; verify inside/outside handles; with key on, alarms sound. Say: I would test the other emergency exits the same as this one.
  • Passenger monitoring devices: internal and external mirrors/cameras clean, adjusted, brackets secure; include student crossover mirrors.
  • First aid kit and body fluid cleanup kit present; seals unbroken.

10) Light check with examiner assistance

Say: Examiner, could you please assist me with my light check.

  • Front: low beams then high-beam indicator; left/right turn and four-ways; clearance/markers; student warning lights (front) and strobe if equipped.
  • Sides: side clearance/markers and side reflectors.
  • Rear: tail, brake, reverse; left/right turn and four-ways; clearance/markers; student warning lights (rear) and strobe if equipped.
  • Stop arm and safety arm: demonstrate full extend and return; dash indicator works.

11) Brake system tests (labeled for study)

  • A. Governor Cut-Out: Air builds to cutoff 120–140 PSI.
  • B. Applied Leak Test: Key on, parking brake released, fully apply foot brake and hold 1 minute; drop is ≤ 3 PSI (single vehicle).
  • C. Low-Air Warning: Warning activates before 55 PSI.
  • D. Spring-Brake Pop-Out: Parking/emergency valve pops 20–45 PSI.
  • E. Parking Brake Check: With parking brake engaged, gently accelerate; bus should not move.
  • F. Service Brake Check: Release parking brake; pull forward ~5 mph; apply service brake to a complete stop; vehicle does not pull.
  • Hydraulic Alternate (if equipped): Pump 3 times, hold 5 seconds; pedal should not move after initial application; confirm power assist.
