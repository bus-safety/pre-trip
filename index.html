1) Air Brake Check (4 parts)

Say: We are parked on level ground. If not, I would chock the wheels.

A) Governor Cut-Out

  • Say: Governor cut-out is 120–140 PSI.
  • Start engine and build air pressure until it reaches cut-out.

B) Applied Leak Test

  • Say: I will shut the engine off, turn the key completely OFF, then back to ON (not AUX) without starting. The engine will remain off for the rest of the air brake test.
  • Say: Air loss is ≤ 3 PSI in 1 minute (single vehicle).
  • Key on, parking brake released, fully apply foot brake and hold 1 minute.

C) Low-Air Warning

  • Say: Low-air warning activates before 55 PSI.
  • Fan the brakes until the warning activates (light and buzzer).

D) Spring-Brake Pop-Out

  • Say: Spring-brake pop-out is 20–45 PSI.
  • Continue fanning brakes until the parking/emergency valve pops out.

2) In-vehicle (key on) checks

  • Parking Brake Check: With parking brake engaged, gently accelerate; bus should not move.
  • Service Brake Check: Release parking brake; pull forward ~5 mph; apply service brake to a complete stop; vehicle does not pull.
  • Lighting indicators: left, right, four-ways, high beam, ABS, DEF if equipped.
  • Emergency equipment: 3 triangles; fire extinguisher charged and mounted; spare fuses or identify circuit breakers.
  • Windshield & traffic monitoring devices: windshield clean/no damage; mirrors clean/adjusted; cameras clean/operable if equipped.
  • Wipers & washers: spray and wipe; arms/blades secure.
  • Heater & defroster: identify controls; demonstrate airflow.
  • Horn(s): works.

3) Passenger area

  • Entry door not damaged; operates smoothly; closes securely from inside.
  • Handrails secure; step light(s) work if equipped; steps clear and tread not loose or worn.
  • Lift (if equipped) has no leaks/damage/missing parts; fully retracted and latched.
  • Emergency exits interior: labeled, not damaged, operate smoothly, close securely. Demonstrate at least one exit; with key on, alarms sound. Say: I would test the other emergency exits the same as this one.
  • Passenger seating: inspect one seat; frame not broken; frame attached to floor; cushion secured. Say: I would check all other seats the same as this one.
  • Passenger monitoring devices: internal and external mirrors/cameras clean, adjusted, brackets secure; include student crossover mirrors.
  • Student warning lights (front & back): activate ambers then reds (and strobe if equipped); dash indicators work; lights operational.
  • Stop arm(s) & safety arm: securely mounted, not damaged; demonstrate extend fully; stop arm lights operational; dash indicator works; safety arm extends.
  • First aid & body fluid kits present; seals not broken.

4) Light check with examiner assistance

Say: Examiner, could you please assist me with my light check.

  • Front: low beams then high-beam indicator; left/right turn and four-ways; clearance/markers.
  • Sides: side clearance/markers.
  • Rear: tail, brake, reverse; left/right turn and four-ways; clearance/markers.

5) Front of Vehicle / Engine Area (Passenger side exterior)

Say: For the exterior inspection, I will inspect the passenger side of the bus.

  • Lenses: clean, proper color, not cracked or loose; housings secure.
  • Fluid levels: coolant between marks; engine oil proper level; power steering fluid level proper; caps present.
  • Fluid & air leaks: no puddles/drips; no leaks from hoses/lines; listen for air leaks.
  • Steering systems: steering box mounted with bolts, no leaks; linkage joints (pitman arm, drag link, tie-rod ends) secured with castle nuts and cotter pins, not loose or worn.

6) Steering Axle (Passenger side)

  • Tires: properly inflated; no cuts/bulges; tread depth ≥ 4/32; valve stem and cap present.
  • Rims: no cracks or weld repairs.
  • Lug nuts: all present and tight; no rust trails/shiny threads.
  • Springs/mounts & air bags & shocks: not cracked, bent, broken, shifted, missing; mounted securely; no leaks.
  • Brake lines or hoses & leaks: not cracked, worn, frayed, or leaking; fittings tight.
  • Brake contaminates: no oil/grease/fluid contamination on visible brake components.

Say: I would check the other steering axle the same as this one.

7) Side of Vehicle (Passenger side)

  • Lenses & reflectors: present, proper color, clean, not cracked or missing; secure.
  • Traffic monitoring devices: external mirrors/cameras clean, adjusted, brackets secure.
  • Battery: I would inspect the battery box on the other side for securement; connections tight; no excessive corrosion; cables not frayed; cover latched.
  • Fuel tank(s)/DEF tank: secured with straps/brackets; caps present/tight; no leaks.
  • Frame(s): no cracks, broken welds, holes, or damage.

8) Rear of Vehicle

  • Lenses & reflectors: present, proper color, clean, not cracked or missing; secure.

Say: I would check the other side the same, with the addition of the battery box and stop arm (stop sign) for securement and damage.
